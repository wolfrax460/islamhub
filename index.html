The Five Pillars of Islam

Shahada (Faith)

Declaration of faith: "There is no any other God for worshipping besides Allah and that Muhammad is his messenger"

Salah (Prayer)

Performing the five daily prayers on their time

Zakat

Giving zakat to those in need.

Sawm

fasting the month of ramadhan.

Hajj

Pilgrimage to Mecca at least once in a lifetime if physically and financially able.

Quranic Foundation

"Righteousness is not that you turn your faces toward the east or the west, but [true] righteousness is [in] one who believes in Allah..." (Quran 2:177)

Tawhid (Oneness of Allah)

Tawhid is the fundamental concept of Islam that affirms the absolute oneness and uniqueness of Allah.

1. Tawhid al-Rububiyyah: Recognizing Allah as the sole Creator, Sustainer, and Controller of the universe.

2. Tawhid al-Uluhiyyah: Directing all acts of worship exclusively to Allah.

3. Tawhid al-Asma' wa al-Sifat: Oneness of Names and Attributes - Affirming the names and attributes of Allah as mentioned in the Quran and authentic Sunnah without distortion or comparison.

"Say, 'He is Allah, [who is] One. Allah, the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born, Nor is there to Him any equivalent.'" (Quran 112:1-4)

Fiqh (Islamic Jurisprudence)

Fiqh is the human understanding and interpretation of Islamic law (Sharia) based on the Quran and Sunnah.

The four major Sunni schools of Fiqh are:

• Hanafi: Founded by Imam Abu Hanifa, prevalent in South Asia, Turkey, and the Balkans

• Maliki: Founded by Imam Malik, dominant in North and West Africa

• Shafi'i: Founded by Imam Shafi'i, common in East Africa, Southeast Asia, and parts of the Middle East

• Hanbali: Founded by Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal, followed in Saudi Arabia and Gulf states

"And We have revealed to you the Book in truth, confirming what came before it of the Scripture and as a criterion over it..." (Quran 5:48)

Sirah (Prophetic Biography)

Sirah refers to the biography of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), detailing his life, character, teachings, and historical context.

Key events in the Sirah include:

• Birth in Mecca (570 CE)

• First revelation at age 40 in Cave Hira

• Migration (Hijrah) to Medina (622 CE)

• Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (628 CE)

• Conquest of Mecca (630 CE)

• Farewell Pilgrimage and sermon (632 CE)

"Indeed in the Messenger of Allah you have an excellent example to follow for whoever hopes in Allah and the Last Day..." (Quran 33:21)
